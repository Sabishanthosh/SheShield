Legal Awareness

Understand your rights, the laws that protect you, and how the complaint process works.

Your Rights

🆓

Free Legal Aid

Every woman has the right to free legal assistance under the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 — regardless of income.

🔒

Privacy Protection

Your identity in sexual harassment / assault cases is legally protected — media and police cannot reveal your name (Sec 354C, IPC).

🌙

Zero FIR

You can file an FIR at any police station, regardless of where the crime occurred. It gets transferred to the right jurisdiction.

👮‍♀️

Women Officer Right

You have the right to request a woman police officer to record your statement, especially in sensitive cases.

🏠

Statement at Home

In certain cases, you can request your statement be recorded at your home or a place of your choice, not the police station.

⏰

No Night Arrest

Women generally cannot be arrested after sunset or before sunrise, except in exceptional circumstances with magistrate permission.

Key Laws That Protect You

Covers unwelcome physical contact, sexual advances, demand for sexual favors, showing pornography, or making sexually colored remarks. Punishment: up to 3 years imprisonment + fine.

Watching, capturing, or sharing images of a woman in a private act without consent. Punishment: 1-3 years (first offense), 3-7 years (repeat offense).

Following, contacting, or monitoring a woman despite clear disinterest (online or offline). Punishment: up to 3 years (first offense), up to 5 years (repeat offense).

Capturing, publishing, or transmitting images of a person's private area without consent. Punishment: up to 3 years + ₹2 lakh fine.

Fraudulent use of another person's identity (fake profiles, password, signature). Punishment: up to 3 years + ₹1 lakh fine.

Publishing or transmitting obscene material electronically, including blackmail using private photos/videos. Punishment: up to 5 years + ₹10 lakh fine (repeat offense).

Protects women from physical, emotional, sexual, and economic abuse by family members. Provides protection orders, residence rights, and monetary relief.

Mandates Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in every workplace with 10+ employees to handle sexual harassment complaints confidentially.

Complaint Process

1

Document Everything

Save screenshots, messages, photos, witness names. Use Evidence Vault to organize records with dates.

2

File the Complaint

Visit any police station (Zero FIR) or file online at cybercrime.gov.in for cyber-related cases. No fee required.

3

Get Acknowledgment

Insist on a written acknowledgment / FIR copy with the complaint number. This is your right — don't leave without it.

4

Investigation

Police investigate, may call you for statement. You can request a woman officer for sensitive cases.

5

Legal Support

Contact District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) for free lawyer support if needed.

6

Follow Up

Track your complaint status using the complaint number. Follow up if no action within reasonable time.
